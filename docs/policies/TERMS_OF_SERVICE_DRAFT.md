## 1. Agreement

These Terms govern access to and use of Tixmo, including the organizer dashboard, event setup, ticketing, checkout, scanner tools, approval review links, asset sharing, support workflows, and related services.

By creating an account, purchasing a ticket, using a scanner, reviewing assets, opening a shared asset link, or otherwise using Tixmo, users agree to these Terms and any additional policies referenced here.

## 2. Who Uses Tixmo

- Organizers create and manage organizations, venues, events, ticket types, orders, staff roles, scanners, approval requests, and asset folders.
- Attendees purchase or receive tickets and may be checked in at an event.
- Staff use dashboard permissions or scanner credentials to operate events.
- Reviewers use public review links to comment on or approve event assets.
- Asset recipients use shared-folder links created by organizers.

## 3. Eligibility and Accounts

Users must be able to form a binding agreement and must provide accurate account and payment information. Users are responsible for protecting passwords, sessions, scanner API keys, public review links, shared asset links, and ticket QR codes.

Tixmo may suspend or restrict accounts, scanner credentials, or public links if there is suspected abuse, unauthorized access, fraud, compromised credentials, payment risk, or violation of these Terms.

## 4. Organizer Responsibilities

Organizers are responsible for:

- Event names, descriptions, dates, venues, capacities, pricing, taxes, fees, age restrictions, accessibility details, cancellation terms, and refund terms.
- Ensuring event listings, assets, marketing, and reviewer materials are accurate and lawful.
- Managing staff access, role assignments, scanner registration, scanner revocation, and gate procedures.
- Honoring consumer protection, ticketing, tax, payout, venue, safety, accessibility, privacy, and communications obligations that apply to their events.
- Handling attendee support, event changes, cancellations, refunds, disputes, and venue-entry decisions unless Tixmo expressly agrees otherwise in writing.

Organizer-specific obligations are also described in `docs/policies/ORGANIZER_TERMS_DRAFT.md`.

## 5. Orders, Tickets, and Entry

Ticket purchase, order confirmation, scanner validation, and attendee entry depend on event configuration, payment status, scanner sync status, and organizer operations.

Tixmo may mark tickets valid, used, cancelled, refunded, or otherwise restricted based on order state, payment state, scanner validation, organizer action, fraud controls, or support repair workflows.

Possession of a ticket, QR code, confirmation email, review link, or asset link does not guarantee event entry, approval, refund, or continued access if the record is invalid, cancelled, refunded, expired, revoked, duplicated, compromised, or otherwise restricted.

## 6. Payments, Fees, Refunds, and Taxes

Payments are processed by Stripe or another configured payment processor. Users may be subject to the payment processor's terms. Tixmo may store transaction references, payment status, order status, webhook event IDs, and related records, but does not intentionally store full card numbers.

Refunds, cancellations, chargebacks, taxes, and fee handling are governed by the event's published refund terms, applicable law, the payment processor's rules, and `docs/policies/REFUND_POLICY_DRAFT.md`.

Before launch, Tixmo must publish the final policy for whether service fees are refundable, who approves refunds, who pays chargeback costs, and which currency, tax, and payout assumptions apply.

## 7. Acceptable Use

Users may not:

- Misrepresent identity, affiliation, event details, pricing, availability, or ticket validity.
- Use Tixmo for unlawful, fraudulent, deceptive, unsafe, infringing, or abusive activity.
- Circumvent role permissions, organization boundaries, scanner credentials, public-token expiry, rate limits, or security controls.
- Upload malware, illegal content, infringing assets, or content they do not have rights to use.
- Resell, scrape, reverse engineer, overload, interfere with, or misuse Tixmo except as allowed by law.
- Share scanner API keys, raw review tokens, raw asset-share tokens, or administrative credentials through insecure channels.
